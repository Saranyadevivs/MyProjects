Welcome to PMSA College

PMSA Pookoya Thangal Memorial Arts and Science College was started during the academic year 2003 -2004 by Villoor Educational and Charitable Trust. The College was named after Pookoya Thangal who was a great Islamic scholar and orator, who dedicated his life to uplift the poor and downtrodden masses of Malabar. He was also a great political leader and President of Indian Union Muslim League. PMSA Pookoya Thangal Memorial Arts and Science College is affiliated to the University of Kerala with the objective of offering university level education in emerging areas of Science, Technology, Commerce and Management and build up the vital infrastructure for the aspirants of higher education especially from the weaker and minority sections of the society.

Our Founder

Late Haji E M Abdul Salam [1942 -2008] was born at Veykkal, Kollam district in an agrarian family. He started his humble beginning as an Arabic teacher in lower primary school in govt service and pursued his studies and completed his university education in securing second rank in MA Arabic in the year 1974 from Kerala University. His vision to provide university education to the minorities and socially backward classes in his home town was materialised in the year 2003 by starting PMSA Pookoya Thangal Memorial Arts and Science College at Kadakkal under the banner of Villoor Educational and Charitable Trust. He christened the institution to commemorate Pookoya Thangal, the great orator and eminent scholar who dedicated his entire life to augment marginised masses of Malabar. He believed that the name is most appropriate for this institution which is also established with the vision to uplift the poor and down trodden masses with quality education.

Infrastructure
The campus life at PMSA is wholesome, easy and fun filled and offers a complete suite of facilities that are very self sufficient to both students and faculties. The College has spacious class rooms, auditoria, laboratories, conference halls, panel rooms and sports facilities. The infrastructure has been augmented to suit the growing needs of students and additional funds have been set aside for effective upkeep.
